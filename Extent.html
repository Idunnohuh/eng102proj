Who is affected?

Housing insecurity affects every working-class American. Every single person who has to work to live can be subject to loosing their house, no matter their current wealth.

The Working Class

Whether they are middle or lower class, any American who has to work to live can be pushed out onto the streets at any time.

The Elderly

Those on fixed incomes, such as the elderly, are especially vulnerable to losing their homes, as politicians and bankers continue to gut social security.

The Already Homeless

People on the streets are far less likely to be able to return to stable housing, continuing the cycle of homelessness.
